Mumbai, 2020

The Beginning

A Tiny Lab.
An Enormous Dream.

It started in a 200 sq ft Mumbai apartment, with a folding table, 40 raw materials sourced from around the world, and an absolute refusal to accept that luxury fragrances should only come from France or the UAE.

Our founder, Aryan Mehta, spent seven years in Grasse — the perfume capital of the world — before returning with a vision: to create world-class fragrances that carried the soul of India without apology. The first batch of Velvet Oud — 50 bottles — sold out in three hours. That was all the confirmation he needed.

What We Stand For

Our Values
Never Compromise.

Every decision we make — from the farmer we source from, to the bottle we choose — is guided by values we formed on day one and have never wavered from.

01
🌿

Purity of Ingredients

We never use synthetic shortcuts for notes that can be sourced naturally. If it takes 8 tonnes of rose petals to produce a single litre of absolute, so be it.

02
🤝

Fair Source Partnerships

Every grower and distiller we work with receives above-market rates and long-term contracts. Their livelihood is as important to us as our own.

03
🎨

Artisan Craftsmanship

No mass production. Every batch is blended by hand, tested over weeks, and approved only when it meets the uncompromising standard of our founder.

04
♻️

Sustainable Packaging

All our bottles are refillable. Our boxes are made from recycled materials. Our shipping packaging is 100% compostable by 2026.

05
🇮🇳

Proudly Indian

We celebrate the richness of Indian perfumery — oud, sandalwood, jasmine, saffron — and place it confidently alongside the finest in the world.

06
💫

Emotion First

We don't create fragrances for the nose. We create them for the heart — for the moment someone smells one of ours and suddenly remembers something beautiful.

The Founder

"I didn't want to build a perfume company. I wanted to build something that made people feel more themselves."

Aryan Mehta spent seven years in Grasse, France before returning to Mumbai with a suitcase full of raw materials and a notebook of formulations that would become FRAGNEST's first six fragrances. Trained under legendary perfumer Henri Belfort, Aryan combines the classical French method with a deep reverence for Indian botanical traditions to create fragrances that are simultaneously global and unmistakably rooted.
